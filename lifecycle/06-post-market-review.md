\# Post-Market Review



The Post-Market Review is the final evaluation stage after a market has been settled and archived.  

Its purpose is to extract durable operational, product, and governance insights that improve future markets.



This review is forward-looking and system-focused.



---



\## Objectives



The post-market review aims to:

\- Identify systemic strengths and weaknesses

\- Validate assumptions made during market design

\- Improve operational playbooks and thresholds

\- Inform governance and product decisions

\- Enhance user trust and market quality over time



---



\## Review Timing



A post-market review is initiated:

\- Within 48â€“72 hours after settlement for standard markets

\- Within 24 hours for high-impact or incident-driven markets

\- After resolution of all disputes and appeals



---



\## Market Performance Summary



\### Market Metadata



Capture:

\- Market ID and category

\- Market duration

\- Total volume and open interest

\- Number of participants

\- Final outcome



This provides baseline context for comparison.



---



\### Volume \& Liquidity Analysis



Assess:

\- Volume distribution over time

\- Liquidity depth during peak activity

\- Maker concentration and churn

\- Spread behavior and volatility



Identify whether liquidity incentives were effective.



---



\## Operational Performance Review



\### Market Creation Quality



Review:

\- Accuracy of initial market framing

\- Resolution criteria clarity

\- Risk flags identified pre-launch

\- Deviations from standard templates



Highlight any preventable issues.



---



\### Live Market Operations



Analyze:

\- Alerts triggered

\- Interventions executed

\- Timing vs severity

\- Escalations required



Determine whether response actions were:

\- Timely

\- Proportionate

\- Effective



---



\### Oracle \& Resolution Performance



Evaluate:

\- Oracle update latency

\- Data consistency

\- Dispute rates

\- Resolution confidence



Oracle issues directly affect platform credibility.



---



\## Risk \& Incident Review



\### Incident Summary



If incidents occurred:

\- Describe the incident

\- Timeline of events

\- Systems affected

\- User impact



If no incidents occurred, document contributing stability factors.



---



\### Root Cause Analysis



For each incident:

\- Immediate cause

\- Contributing factors

\- Control failures

\- Detection gaps



Avoid individual blame. Focus on system improvements.



---



\## User Impact \& Trust Signals



Review:

\- Support tickets and complaints

\- Dispute participation

\- Social or community feedback

\- Net promoter signals



User trust degradation is treated as a first-class risk.



---



\## Governance \& Policy Feedback



Identify:

\- Parameters that require adjustment

\- Market types that need stricter controls

\- Oracle providers needing review

\- Playbooks needing updates



Flag issues for governance discussion where applicable.



---



\## Action Items \& Ownership



Each review must produce:

\- Clear action items

\- Assigned owners

\- Target completion dates

\- Follow-up verification



Unassigned actions are considered incomplete.



---



\## Documentation \& Knowledge Base Updates



Update:

\- Runbooks

\- Monitoring thresholds

\- Market templates

\- Internal training materials



Operational learning must be institutionalized.



---



\## Review Sign-Off



A post-market review is considered complete when:

\- Findings are documented

\- Action items are assigned

\- Required governance items are logged

\- Artifacts are stored and indexed



---



\## Continuous Improvement Loop



Insights from post-market reviews feed into:

\- Future market curation

\- Risk assessment frameworks

\- Alerting strategies

\- Incentive design



This closes the operational feedback loop and improves market quality over time.
